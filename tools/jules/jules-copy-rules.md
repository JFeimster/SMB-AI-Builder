# Jules Copy Rules

## Core Voice
- Be direct.
- Use short blocks.
- Write for SMB owners and operators.
- Do not sound like enterprise SaaS.
- Practical builder tone.
- Anti-hype.
- No soft SaaS fluff.

## Approved Phrases
- Audit the mess first.
- Score readiness.
- Pick the safest pilot.
- Build an AI agent plan your business can survive.
- Stop automating broken workflows.

## Banned Phrases
- Avoid banned phrases like "unleash", "revolutionize", "game-changer", etc.
- Do not promise automation outcomes.
- Do not create fake urgency.

## CTA Rules
- Make the CTA obvious.
- Position the GPT as a planning, audit, readiness, and pilot-design layer.

## Safety Language
- Make the safety stance clear.
- Do not say AI replaces qualified human review.
- Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review.
- This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice.

## Claims Rules
- Do not invent proof.
- Do not overstate what the GPT does.
- Do not invent or imply:
  - Fake testimonials
  - Fake reviews
  - Fake ratings
  - Fake logos
  - Fake case studies
  - Fake user counts
  - Fake revenue numbers
  - Fake guarantees
  - Fake compliance claims
  - Fake partner relationships
  - Fake certifications
  - Fake benchmarks
  - Fake integrations
  - Unsupported competitor claims

## Rewrite Rules
- Simplify and shorten copy where necessary while retaining accuracy and directness.

## Final Copy QA
- Ensure copy aligns with the core voice and passes safety and claims rules.
