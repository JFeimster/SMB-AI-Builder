# Jules Copy Rules

## Core Voice
- Be direct.
- Use short blocks.
- Write for SMB owners and operators.
- Do not sound like enterprise SaaS.
- Keep the tone practical, operator-friendly, anti-hype, safety-aware, and clear.
- Position the GPT as a planning, audit, readiness, and pilot-design layer.
- Do not position AI as a replacement for qualified human review.
- Do not overstate what the GPT does.

## Approved Phrases
Use these phrases where they fit naturally:

- Stop automating broken workflows.
- Audit the mess first.
- Audit the mess before you wire it into your business.
- Score readiness.
- Score the workflow before AI touches it.
- Know what to automate, what to clean up, and what should stay human.
- Pick the safest pilot.
- Build a pilot plan your business can survive.
- Build an AI agent plan your business can survive.
- Plan the workflow before choosing the tool.
- Use AI where it helps, keep people in the loop where it matters.

## Banned Phrases
Avoid these phrases unless they appear inside a rule, checklist, or banned-phrase explanation:

- unlock your potential
- revolutionize your workflow
- revolutionize
- seamless experience
- seamless
- game-changing
- game-changer
- cutting-edge
- transform your business
- leverage AI
- AI-powered magic
- effortless automation
- fully autonomous business
- set it and forget it
- unleash

## CTA Rules
- Make the CTA obvious.
- Use action-oriented CTA language.
- Do not bury CTA links.
- Use the primary GPT CTA for launch actions: `https://chatgpt.com/g/g-6a3cac9b87f08191af3f98ab42ad55ae-smb-ai-workflow-agent-builder`.
- Approved CTA examples:
  - Launch SMB AI Workflow & Agent Builder
  - Audit My First Workflow
  - Score My Automation Readiness
  - Build My AI Agent Pilot Plan
  - Find What To Automate First
- CTA copy must not promise approval, savings, compliance, automation outcomes, revenue outcomes, or guaranteed performance.

## Safety Language
Use safety language when copy discusses sensitive workflows, private data, customer communication, finance, legal, HR, medical, lending, underwriting, tax, accounting, or compliance.

Approved disclaimer:

> This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice. Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review.

Use practical safety guidance:

- Draft-only mode where risk is high.
- Human approval before action.
- Limited permissions.
- Test data first.
- Audit logs.
- Escalation rules.
- Rollback plan.
- Qualified human review.

## Claims Rules
Do not invent proof.
Do not create fake urgency.
Do not imply official relationships that are not configured or documented.
Do not claim the GPT performs actions beyond planning, auditing, readiness scoring, workflow design, and pilot planning.

Do not invent or imply:

- Fake testimonials
- Fake reviews
- Fake ratings
- Fake logos
- Fake case studies
- Fake user counts
- Fake revenue numbers
- Fake guarantees
- Fake compliance claims
- Fake partner relationships
- Fake certifications
- Fake benchmarks
- Fake integrations
- Unsupported competitor claims

## Rewrite Rules
When rewriting copy:

- Replace vague AI promises with concrete workflow planning language.
- Replace enterprise SaaS fluff with operator-ready instructions.
- Replace hype with risk-aware clarity.
- Replace fake urgency with practical next steps.
- Shorten bloated paragraphs.
- Keep CTAs visible and specific.
- Keep the safety stance clear.
- Make the page useful even if the user never clicks the CTA.

## Final Copy QA
Before finalizing copy, verify:

- [ ] The copy is direct and practical.
- [ ] The copy is written for SMB owners and operators.
- [ ] The copy avoids banned phrases.
- [ ] Approved phrases are used where helpful.
- [ ] CTA language is obvious and action-oriented.
- [ ] No fake proof was added.
- [ ] No fake urgency was added.
- [ ] No automation outcome is promised.
- [ ] The GPT is positioned as a planning, audit, readiness, and pilot-design layer.
- [ ] The safety stance is clear where sensitive workflows appear.
- [ ] The copy does not say AI replaces qualified human review.
