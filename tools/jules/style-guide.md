# Jules Style Guide

## Visual Style
- Neobrutalist design direction.
- Futurist fintech aesthetic.
- Cyberpunk inspiration where relevant.
- High contrast and practical builder aesthetic.
- No soft vague AI visuals.

## Color Language
- **Background:** Ink black.
- **Cards/Blocks:** Off-white.
- **Accents:**
  - Acid lime
  - Cyber pink
  - Electric cyan
  - Signal orange
  - Violet

## Layout Rules
- Neobrutalist blocks.
- Thick borders.
- Hard offset shadows.
- Dashboard panels.
- Scorecard motifs.
- Terminal-style snippets.
- Oversized headings.
- Mobile-first layouts.

## Component Patterns
- Monospace labels.
- Clear structural hierarchy.
- Plain semantic HTML structures for static pages.

## Copy Voice
- Direct, no-BS, operator-friendly, anti-hype, practical, safety-aware, clear, concise.
- Anti-corporate SaaS tone.
- No generic chatbot copy.
- No fake urgency.
- No fake social proof.
- No promises of automation outcomes.

## Approved Phrases
- Stop automating broken workflows.
- Audit the mess before you wire it into your business.
- Score the workflow before AI touches it.
- Know what to automate, what to clean up, and what should stay human.
- Build a pilot plan your business can actually survive.
- Use AI where it helps, keep people in the loop where it matters.
- Plan the workflow before choosing the tool.

## Banned Phrases
- unlock your potential
- revolutionize your workflow
- seamless experience
- game-changing
- cutting-edge
- transform your business
- leverage AI
- AI-powered magic
- effortless automation
- fully autonomous business
- set it and forget it

## CTA Language
- Must be direct and action-oriented.
- Example defaults:
  - Audit My First Workflow
  - Score My Automation Readiness
  - Build My AI Agent Pilot Plan
  - Find What To Automate First
- Must have high CTA contrast against backgrounds.

## Safety Language
- **Disclaimer:** "This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice. Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review."
- For sensitive workflows (finance, legal, HR, medical, etc.), always recommend:
  - Draft-only mode
  - Human approval before action
  - Limited permissions
  - Test data first
  - Audit logs
  - Escalation rules
  - Rollback plan
  - Qualified human review

## Accessibility Style Rules
- Accessible button labels.
- Visible focus states.
- Sufficient color contrast (especially on neobrutalist offset elements and accents).
- Semantic landmarks.
- No text trapped in images.
- Meaningful alt text for images; hide decorative visuals from assistive tech.
- ARIA used only where absolutely needed.
