# Skill: Drive Knowledge Organizer

## Purpose
Plans the organization of knowledge base documents within Google Drive.

## Use When
When setting up a team knowledge base, SOP repository, or training library in Google Drive.

## Inputs Needed
- Types of knowledge documents (SOPs, policies, templates)
- Target audience (internal team, contractors, clients)
- Search and retrieval requirements

## Outputs
- A structured knowledge base architecture
- Naming conventions for files
- Tagging or indexing strategies

## Procedure
1. Categorize the knowledge domains of the business.
2. Propose a root folder structure for the knowledge base.
3. Establish clear, consistent file naming conventions.
4. Suggest a method for keeping documents updated (e.g., review cycles).
5. Ensure safety and compliance with data rules.

## Safety / Human Review
- Do not suggest storing sensitive HR or financial data in open folders.
- No compliance, legal, or HR advice.

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Naming conventions are clear and searchable?
- [ ] Structure separates public/team/private knowledge?
- [ ] Avoids HR/Legal advice claims?

## Example Prompt
```
We want to create a central repository for all our company SOPs in Google Drive. What is the best way to organize this so our new hires can easily find what they need without getting lost?
```
