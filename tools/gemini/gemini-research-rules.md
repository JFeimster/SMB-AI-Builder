# Gemini Research Rules

When asking Gemini to perform external research, competitive analysis, or fact-checking, follow these rules to ensure high-quality, trustworthy outputs.

## 1. Use Current Sources
When researching rapidly changing topics (like new AI models, pricing updates, or feature releases), require Gemini to prioritize the most recent information available and verify the date of the claims.

## 2. Prefer Primary Sources
Always prioritize documentation, official websites, API references, and direct company statements over secondary blogs, aggregators, or opinion pieces.

## 3. Cite Claims
Ensure any specific numbers, features, or competitive claims in the research output are backed by a citation or direct link to the source. Do not accept standalone facts without a traceable origin.

## 4. No Unsupported Product Claims
Research should inform strategy, not write fiction. Under no circumstances should research be turned into unsupported product claims for the SMB AI Builder. Do not invent:
- Benchmarks
- Direct integrations that don't exist
- User counts
- Revenue impacts
- Fake case studies
- Fake reviews or testimonials

## 5. Neutral Competitive Framing
When researching competitors (e.g., Zapier, Make), maintain a balanced, objective view. Frame comparisons as a decision guide. Do not attack competitors. Position the GPT accurately as a planning and readiness layer designed to be used *before* building with those tools.
