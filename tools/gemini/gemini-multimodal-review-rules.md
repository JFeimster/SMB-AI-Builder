# Gemini Multimodal Review Rules

When passing images, mockups, or screenshots to Gemini for visual critique, instruct it to evaluate the image based on these criteria.

## 1. Visual Consistency & Style
- Does the design adhere to the neobrutalist, cyberpunk, and futurist fintech aesthetic?
- Look for high contrast, thick borders, hard offset shadows, ink black backgrounds, and vivid accent colors.

## 2. Hero Clarity
- Is the main value proposition immediately obvious?
- Does the hero section clearly explain what the tool does (e.g., audits workflows, scores readiness)?

## 3. CTA Visibility
- Are the primary Call-To-Action buttons clearly visible?
- Do they stand out from the background?
- Do the labels use direct terminology (e.g., "Audit My First Workflow", "Score My Automation Readiness")?

## 4. Contrast & Accessibility
- Is there sufficient contrast between text and background?
- Are interactive elements distinctly styled so they don't blend into static text?
- Are focus states visible (if reviewing an interactive state)?

## 5. Mobile Layout
- If reviewing a mobile screenshot, is the text readable without zooming?
- Are touch targets appropriately sized?
- Is there excessive horizontal scrolling or overflowing elements?

## 6. Crowding & Readability
- Is there adequate whitespace between sections?
- Are font sizes legible across different device breakpoints?
- Is the layout cluttered with unnecessary decorative elements?

## 7. Trust Signals & Honesty
- Ensure that the design does not include fake trust signals, such as fabricated user counts, fake star ratings, or unauthorized client logos.
- If a sensitive workflow is depicted, ensure the required safety disclaimer is visible near the relevant section.
