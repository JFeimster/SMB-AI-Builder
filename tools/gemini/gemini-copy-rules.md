# Gemini Copy Rules

When asking Gemini to review, rewrite, or draft copy for the SMB AI Builder project, enforce these strict tone and style constraints.

## 1. Anti-Hype Tone
The copy must be direct, practical, and operator-friendly. We do not sell magic; we sell systematic workflow audits and safe pilot plans.

**Do NOT use these phrases (or similar):**
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Revolutionize your workflow"
- "Seamless experience"
- "Game-changing"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Transform your business"
- "Leverage AI"

## 2. Operator Language
Speak to the audience (SMB owners, operations managers, freelancers, lean agencies) using practical terminology. Use checklists, placeholders, and direct instructions rather than fluffy deployment theory. Focus on real-world constraints like time, risk, and human approval.

## 3. Truthfulness & No Fake Claims
Never invent data. Copy must not include:
- Fake testimonials or reviews.
- Invented ratings or user counts.
- Fake case studies or revenue claims.
- Fabricated benchmarks.
- Unsupported integrations.

## 4. Safety First
If the copy discusses customer-facing, financial, or compliance workflows, it must recommend draft-only mode, human approval, or exception review.

**Required Disclaimer for Sensitive Pages:**
> "This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice. Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review."

## 5. Direct CTAs
CTAs should be clear and action-oriented. Examples:
- "Audit My First Workflow"
- "Score My Automation Readiness"
- "Build My AI Agent Pilot Plan"
- "Find What To Automate First"
