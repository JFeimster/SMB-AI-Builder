# Claude Longform Review Rules

When reviewing or drafting long-form content (such as Workflow Audit Pages or Comparison Pages), ensure the following criteria are met:

## 1. Structure
- Follow specific required structures for page types.
  - **Workflow Audit Pages:** Required H1 format is "Audit Your [Workflow] Before Automating It With AI". Must include: Why it's tempting to automate, What can go wrong, What the GPT checks, Readiness factors, Suggested safe pilot, Human review checkpoints, CTA, and 3-5 FAQs.
  - **Comparison Pages:** Must be balanced, framed as a decision guide, never attack competitors (Zapier, Make, etc.). Position the GPT as a planning/readiness layer before building. Must include: 'Use this when...', 'Audit first when...' sections, a comparison table/card grid, a CTA band, and 4-6 FAQs.

## 2. Redundancy
- Eliminate repetitive points. Consolidate similar arguments and ensure every section adds unique, actionable value.

## 3. Search Intent
- Align content with the user's practical need to audit, prepare, and plan safely rather than just "automate everything." Answer the 'how' and 'why'.

## 4. CTA Clarity
- Ensure CTAs are unambiguous and use core language (e.g., "Build My AI Agent Pilot Plan"). Verify that CTA links point to the primary URL.

## 5. Internal Links
- Verify relative paths based on depth rules. Ensure internal linking builds a coherent site structure without broken links.

## 6. Safety Language
- Check that the required explicit disclaimer is present for sensitive workflows. Ensure no output is framed as professional advice (legal, financial, HR, etc.). Recommend human-in-the-loop safeguards.
