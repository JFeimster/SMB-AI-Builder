# Claude Copy Rules

## Tone and Voice
- **Direct & No-BS:** Cut the fluff. Speak directly to operators, small business owners, and lean teams.
- **Workflow-First:** Focus on the practical mechanics of the process (e.g., how to audit, score readiness, and plan) rather than theoretical AI capabilities.
- **Operator-Friendly:** Use checklists, clear placeholders, and actionable steps.

## Words to Avoid (Anti-Hype List)
Do not use these vague or overpromising words:
- Unlock your potential
- Revolutionize your workflow
- Seamless experience
- Game-changing
- Cutting-edge
- Transform your business
- Leverage AI
- Magic
- Effortless

## Claims and Honesty
- **Do Not Invent:** Never make up testimonials, ratings, logos, revenue claims, compliance claims, case studies, or guarantees.
- **Specific Detail:** Prefer concrete, useful details over generalized praise.

## Disclaimers and Safety
- For sensitive workflows (e.g., money, compliance, customer-facing), always include this exact disclaimer visibly near the relevant CTA:
  > "This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice. Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review."
- Recommend safeguards such as draft-only mode, human approval before action, audit logs, test data first, and rollback plans.

## Formatting
- Use bullet points, bold text for emphasis, and clear headings.
- Ensure CTA language aligns with core actions (e.g., "Audit My First Workflow", "Score My Automation Readiness").
