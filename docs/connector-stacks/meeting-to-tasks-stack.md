# Meeting-to-Tasks Stack

## What This Stack Is For
This stack ensures that actionable items discussed in meetings are immediately captured, assigned, and tracked, rather than disappearing into notebook margins or lost memories.

## Minimum Stack
- **Calendar**: To schedule and track when meetings occur.
- **Gmail**: For sending follow-up summaries and task assignments to attendees.
- **ClickUp / Notion (or Asana/Trello)**: The project management tool where tasks live.
- **GPT**: To parse raw meeting notes or transcripts into structured tasks.

## Expanded Stack
*(Add these only after the minimum stack is working reliably)*
- **Otter.ai / Fathom / Fireflies**: To automatically record and transcribe the meetings.
- **Zapier/Make**: To automatically push GPT-formatted tasks directly into ClickUp/Notion.
- **Slack**: For instant notification of new task assignments to the team.

## Data Flow
1. A meeting occurs (scheduled via **Calendar**).
2. Raw notes are taken manually (or a transcript is generated by an expanded tool).
3. The raw text is fed to **GPT**.
4. **GPT** extracts the key decisions and drafts a bulleted list of actionable tasks with suggested assignees.
5. A human reviews the list and enters the approved tasks into **ClickUp / Notion**.
6. A summary email is sent to attendees via **Gmail** confirming the next steps.

## What AI Can Draft
- Concise summaries of meandering meeting transcripts.
- Distinct, actionable tasks extracted from conversational notes.
- Draft follow-up emails to stakeholders outlining what was decided.

## What AI Should Not Decide
- Final task deadlines or severity/priority levels.
- Reassigning tasks to team members without a manager's review.
- Making strategic project decisions based on unresolved meeting debates.

## Risk Level
**Low to Medium**. While assigning the wrong task is annoying, it is usually caught quickly by the team. Human review of the extracted tasks prevents confusion.

## Setup Checklist
- [ ] Establish a standard project/board in ClickUp/Notion for "Meeting Action Items".
- [ ] Decide how raw notes will be captured (manual typing vs. transcription tool).
- [ ] Write a GPT prompt that forces output into a specific format (e.g., "Task - Assignee - Deadline").
- [ ] Test the process with an internal team meeting before using it on client calls.

## Starter GPT Prompt
```text
Here is the rough transcript/notes from my recent meeting:
[Paste Notes Here]

Please provide two things:
1. A 3-sentence executive summary of the meeting.
2. A bulleted list of Action Items. For each action item, state what needs to be done, who is responsible (if mentioned), and the deadline (if mentioned). Do not make up deadlines if they are not in the notes.
```

## Success Criteria
- "Who is doing what?" is never a question after a meeting.
- Follow-up emails are sent within an hour of the meeting ending.
- The project management tool accurately reflects the current state of work.
