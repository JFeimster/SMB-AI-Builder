# Automation Opportunity Matrix

## Purpose
This matrix helps you prioritize what to automate first. Stop automating broken workflows. Score the workflow before AI touches it to decide which one provides the best ROI with the lowest risk.

## When to use this matrix
Use this matrix when you have a list of several potential workflows to automate, but aren't sure where to begin. It helps you rank automation opportunities based on business impact and readiness.

## Opportunity Scoring Factors

### Frequency
How often does this workflow happen? (e.g., Daily, Weekly, Monthly)

### Time Saved
How much manual time would automation save per instance?

### Revenue Impact
Does this directly impact sales, billing, or lead capture?

### Customer Experience Impact
Will this make the customer happier (faster response) or frustrated (robotic replies)?

### Risk
If this breaks, what happens? (Low = internal delay; High = compliance breach, lost money).
*Note: This GPT supports workflow planning and pilot design. It is not legal, financial, tax, HR, lending, accounting, medical, underwriting, or compliance advice. Sensitive decisions should stay under qualified human review.*

### Data Readiness
Is the data clean, structured, and accessible?

### Tool Readiness
Do your tools connect easily (APIs/Zapier)?

### Process Clarity
Is the current manual process documented and consistent?

### Ownership Clarity
Who is responsible if it breaks?

### Human Review Needs
Does it require human oversight before acting?

## Scoring Model
Score each factor from 1 (Low/Poor) to 5 (High/Excellent).
- High Frequency, High Time Saved, Low Risk, High Readiness = **Top Priority**
- Low Frequency, High Risk, Low Readiness = **Do Not Automate**

## Recommendation Categories
- **Automate now:** High value, high readiness, low risk.
- **Clean up first:** High value, low readiness (messy process/data).
- **Keep human:** High risk, requires complex professional judgment.
- **Revisit later:** Low value, low frequency.

## Ranking Workflow
1. List 3-5 workflows you want to automate.
2. Fill out the table above for each.
3. Identify the 'Automate now' candidates.
4. Choose the *easiest, lowest-risk* one as your first pilot.

## How to choose top 1–3 workflows
Pick workflows that are:
1. Painful enough that the team cares.
2. Simple enough that you can build a pilot in a few days.
3. Low-risk enough that a failure won't hurt the business.

## What not to automate first
- Payroll.
- Complex custom legal contracts.
- Angry customer escalations.
- Anything where the underlying data is a mess.
