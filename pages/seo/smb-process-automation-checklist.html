SMB Process Automation Checklist: Clean Up Before You Automate

Learn everything you need to know about SMB process automation checklist. Stop automating broken workflows. Audit one messy process, score its automation readiness, and leave with a safe AI agent pilot plan before you waste money on tools, devs, or chaos with a login screen.

Checklist overview

An SMB process automation checklist is your defense against automating chaos. Before selecting AI tools or writing scripts, this checklist forces an objective review of the current workflow. It is designed to separate processes that are genuinely ready for a pilot from those that require fundamental operational cleanup. Following this checklist ensures you build on a solid foundation, rather than automating broken rules.

Do-not-automate-yet signs

The checklist helps you spot major red flags. If a process relies heavily on 'tribal knowledge' (unstated rules only one person knows), has highly inconsistent inputs (messy data), or lacks clear ownership, it is not ready. Automating these processes will amplify the confusion. The checklist flags these issues so you can focus on standardizing the process first.

Ready-to-pilot signs

Conversely, the checklist identifies the green lights. A workflow is ready when the steps are clearly documented, the required data inputs are standardized and accessible, the exceptions and edge cases are known, and the success metrics are definable. Processes meeting these criteria are prime candidates for a safe, human-reviewed automation pilot.

Human review checklist

A critical section of the checklist focuses on human oversight. It verifies that you have defined clear escalation paths, established review checkpoints (like draft-only modes), and assigned a specific human owner to monitor the pilot's outputs. Checking these boxes ensures the automation will operate safely within your business environment.
