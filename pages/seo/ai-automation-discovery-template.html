Discovery questions

A robust AI automation discovery template is built on sharp, interrogative questions. It moves past 'what do you want to automate?' to ask: 'What triggers this process?', 'Where does the data live?', and 'What is the exact penalty if this step fails?' These questions expose the structural reality of the workflow, revealing dependencies and edge cases that casual observation misses.

What to capture

The template must capture the full operational context. You must document the step-by-step actions, the specific software tools involved, the human owners of each step, and the required inputs (e.g., specific CRM fields or document types). Crucially, the template must capture the 'unhappy paths'—the documented rules for what happens when data is missing or a customer complains.

Scoring model

An effective template integrates a scoring model to evaluate readiness objectively. By scoring categories like process clarity, data quality, and risk level, the template provides a quantitative assessment. This scoring mechanism prevents teams from pursuing technically feasible but operationally dangerous automations, ensuring resources are focused on mature workflows.

Client/internal deliverables

The completed discovery template translates directly into actionable deliverables. The captured information forms the basis for a workflow map, a readiness report, and the blueprint for the initial pilot plan. Whether used by an internal operations team or an external agency, the template standardizes the planning process and ensures a focus on safe, measurable implementation.
