Make vs AI Agent Workflows: Map the Process Before You Build

Compare complex visual scenario automation with AI-assisted workflow support to choose the right path for your SMB.

Scenario Automation vs. AI-Assisted Workflow Support

Make (formerly Integromat) is a powerful visual automation platform. It excels at building "scenarios"—complex, multi-branching workflows that route structured data between hundreds of apps using filters, routers, and iterators.

AI-assisted workflows, on the other hand, introduce an LLM (Large Language Model) to handle steps that require reasoning, summarization, or working with unstructured data (like raw text, emails, or PDFs).

When Make-Style Automation Fits

Make is ideal for workflows that are purely logical and data-driven.

  • Syncing complex e-commerce order data across an ERP, a CRM, and a fulfillment provider based on strict inventory rules.
  • Listening for webhook events from a payment processor and routing receipts to specific folders based on transaction amounts.

Use this when...

You have complex branching logic (if A, go to path 1; if B, go to path 2) and all data inputs are highly structured and predictable.

When an AI Agent May Fit

An AI agent (or an LLM step within an automation) is necessary when rigid logic fails.

  • Reading a messy, unformatted vendor invoice and extracting the line items into JSON.
  • Reviewing a customer complaint email to determine urgency and sentiment before routing it to a support tier.

Use this when...

The input data is unstructured, requires comprehension, or you need to generate a human-like response.

Workflow Clarity, Data Readiness, Risk, and Review Rules

Before building in Make or deploying an AI agent, you must assess:

  • Workflow Clarity: Can you draw the entire process on a whiteboard? If not, do not start building.
  • Data Readiness: Do you have access to the necessary APIs, and is your data clean?
  • Risk & Review: What happens if the automation fails or the AI hallucinates? Establish strict human-in-the-loop review rules for sensitive actions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between scenario automation and AI-assisted workflows?

Scenario automation relies on complex visual logic mapping of strict triggers and actions. AI-assisted workflows incorporate an LLM to interpret unstructured data or provide reasoning within that process.

When does Make-style automation fit best?

It fits best when you have multi-step processes with clear branching logic (routers, filters) and structured data inputs across many different apps.

When does an AI agent fit into a workflow?

An AI agent fits when the workflow requires reading documents, understanding intent from customer emails, or generating human-readable drafts that a strict script cannot handle.

Should I use both?

Often, yes. Make is excellent at handling the 'plumbing' (fetching and moving data), while the AI agent acts as the 'brain' within the flow.
