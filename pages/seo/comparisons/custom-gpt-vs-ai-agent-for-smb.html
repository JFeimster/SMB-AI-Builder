Custom GPT vs AI Agent for SMB Workflows

Know the boundaries between a planning assistant and an action-taking agent to keep your business safe.

Planning Assistant vs. Action-Taking Agent

A Custom GPT is typically confined to a chat interface. It acts as an advisor, reading documents you provide and answering questions. It requires a human to copy its output and paste it elsewhere to take action.

An AI Agent is integrated into your software stack. It doesn't just suggest a response to a customer email; it connects to your inbox, drafts the response, and (if permitted) sends it autonomously.

Custom GPT Use Cases

  • Internal Knowledge Base: Uploading employee handbooks so staff can query HR policies.
  • Content Drafting: Providing a GPT with brand guidelines to draft social media posts or blog outlines.
  • Workflow Auditing: Using a specialized GPT (like the SMB AI Workflow Builder) to map out business processes and assess risk before building anything.

Use this when...

You need reasoning, brainstorming, or synthesis, but the final action requires human execution.

AI Agent Use Cases

  • Inbox Management: Triaging support tickets and automatically refunding orders below a certain dollar threshold based on policy.
  • Data Entry: Monitoring a shared folder for PDF invoices, extracting the data, and entering it directly into QuickBooks.

Use this when...

You want the system to execute tasks autonomously to save time, and you have established clear guardrails to prevent catastrophic errors.

Permission Boundaries and Human Handoff Rules

The primary risk difference is permission. A Custom GPT cannot delete your database. An AI agent, if poorly configured, might.

Always establish strict Human Handoff Rules. For example: An AI agent can draft a quote for a client, but a human must click 'Approve' before it is sent.

Comparison Table

Feature Custom GPT AI Agent
Autonomy Low (Waits for user prompts) High (Runs in the background)
System Access Isolated (Chat interface) Integrated (APIs, Databases)
Risk Level Low (Bad advice must be manually acted upon) High (Can execute bad actions automatically)

Audit first when...

  • You are considering giving an AI system write-access to any of your core databases or communication channels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a Custom GPT and an AI Agent?

A Custom GPT acts primarily as an interactive assistant or consultant within a chat interface. An AI Agent is connected to your business systems and can autonomously take actions.

What are Custom GPT use cases?

Custom GPTs are great for employee onboarding, drafting content, brainstorming, or acting as an internal knowledge base querying tool.

What are AI Agent use cases?

AI Agents are used for active workflows: qualifying inbound leads, parsing vendor invoices, or managing customer support routing.

Which one should an SMB start with?

SMBs should almost always start with a Custom GPT to pilot the AI's reasoning capabilities safely before giving an AI Agent permission to take actions in live systems.
