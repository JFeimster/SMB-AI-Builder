When automation helps

Quote follow-up automation is highly effective for maintaining momentum in the sales cycle. Sales teams often forget to nudge prospects after sending a proposal, leading to stalled deals. Automation ensures consistent, polite reminders are sent at the right intervals, freeing up reps to focus on active negotiations rather than administrative tracking.

Pricing/context risk

However, following up on quotes involves significant context risk. The tone and urgency of a follow-up should vary drastically depending on the deal size, the prospect's previous engagement, and any custom pricing discussed. An automated system that blindly sends a generic 'just checking in' message can appear tone-deaf and jeopardize a nuanced negotiation.

Review rules

Before implementing quote follow-up automation, establish strict review rules. For standard, low-value quotes, a fully automated sequence might be acceptable. But for custom proposals or high-value contracts, the automation should default to drafting a reminder for the account owner to review, ensuring the nuance of the deal is respected.

Safe pilot

A safe pilot for quote follow-up begins with your most standardized offerings. Set up the automation to draft reminder emails for open quotes under a certain dollar threshold. Monitor the response rates and ensure the CRM status updates correctly. Only after proving success with standard quotes should you consider expanding the automation to more complex deals.
