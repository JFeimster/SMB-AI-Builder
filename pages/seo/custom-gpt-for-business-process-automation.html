What a Custom GPT can help plan

Using a Custom GPT for business process automation planning acts as a strategic co-pilot. It excels at consuming messy information—rough SOPs, meeting transcripts, or CRM notes—and structuring it into a coherent workflow map. It helps identify gaps in logic, suggests necessary human review checkpoints, and drafts the foundational blueprint for how an automated agent should behave.

What it should not control directly

It is crucial to understand boundaries. A Custom GPT used for planning should not be granted direct, unsupervised access to execute actions in your core systems. It should not independently alter financial records, send unreviewed customer emails, or change critical CRM data. Its role is diagnostic and prescriptive, not autonomous execution.

Workflow audit outputs

When fed process details, the GPT should generate specific audit outputs. These include a clear step-by-step breakdown of the current state, a readiness score assessing the viability of automation, and a prioritized list of areas requiring operational cleanup. These outputs provide the clarity needed before engaging developers or purchasing automation platforms.

Blueprint outputs

Beyond auditing, the planning GPT generates the AI agent blueprint. This document defines the proposed automation's role, expected inputs and outputs, strict behavioral guardrails, and testing scenarios. This blueprint serves as the definitive specification document, ensuring that when you do build the automation, it aligns perfectly with your business requirements and safety standards.
